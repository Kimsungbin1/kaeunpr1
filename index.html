First Aid Methods

Burns

Burns are tissue damage brought on by heat, chemicals, electricity, radiation or the sun. Nearly half a million Americans seek medical care for accidental burns each year. First-degree burns, and most second-degree burns, heal with at-home treatments. Third-degree burns can be life-threatening and require specialized medical care.

Sprains

Ligaments are fibrous, elastic bands of tissue that connect and stabilize the bones. An ankle sprain is a common, painful injury that occurs when one or more of the ankle ligaments is stretched beyond the normal range of motion. Sprains can occur as a result of sudden twisting, turning or rolling movements.

Brush Burns

A brush burn, also known as an abrasion or road rash, is a type of skin injury that occurs when the skin is scraped or rubbed against a rough surface, such as concrete, asphalt, or gravel. This type of injury is common in activities like bicycling, skateboarding, or falling on a rough surface.

Stab wound

A stab wound is a type of penetrating injury caused by a sharp, pointed object, such as a knife, screwdriver, or other sharp instrument, that pierces the skin and underlying tissues.
